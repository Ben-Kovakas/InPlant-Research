## PRIMARY: Selig Enterprises — the building set

**Selig Enterprises** is a family-owned, privately-held Atlanta commercial real estate company **founded in 1918**, with a portfolio of **>15M sqft** (retail, industrial, office, mixed-use) across the Southeast and **~4M sqft of retail** — much of it **older intown Atlanta shopping centers** Selig has held for decades. ([Selig — About](https://seligenterprises.com/about-us/), [Selig — History](https://seligenterprises.com/our-history/)) This is the "one company, a cluster of buildings, wants the money" actor the pitch needs.

**Why Selig's stock is intervention GOLD:** unlike downtown towers, Selig's intown retail centers are **low-rise / single-story with very large flat roofs** sitting on parcels with **large surface parking lots**. That geometry maximizes *every* intervention at once: big roof → big PV array (kW) + big green-roof area (gallons) + big impervious-runoff story; big surface lot → the **lattice / solar-canopy** intervention finally has somewhere to go. A 1960s–70s un-retrofitted retail roof is also a textbook **low ENERGY STAR "before."**

## ALTERNATE B: City of Atlanta municipal portfolio (precedent, not actor)

- **Single owner**, CBEEO-covered (municipal >10,000 sqft), and owns the **literal precedent building** in our regulatory chain.
- **Atlanta City Hall — 68 Mitchell St SW** (1930): a **3,000 sqft pilot green roof** was installed; structure designed for ~**186 psf** and the green roof added only ~**58 psf** → **no structural reinforcement required** — the single strongest "it gets permitted / it's safe on an old building" proof in the deck. ([Greenroofs.com](https://www.greenroofs.com/projects/atlanta-city-hall-pilot-green-roof/))
- **Weakness:** a municipality can't claim the 30% ITC the way a private owner can. Use City Hall as the **named precedent** inside the Selig pitch, not as the actor.
